Best Tourist Attractions in Malaysia's City Area

1. Petronas Twin Towers, Kuala Lumpur

The tallest twin towers in the world, the Petronas reach an impressive 452 meters high up into the clouds. The towers are 88 floors tall and have an impressive total of 76 elevators. Built using reinforced concrete, steel, and glass, the two towers are connected to each other by a double skybridge on the 41st and 42nd floors. Visitors can make their way up here for stunning views of KL and the 6.9-hectare KLCC Park below-the views are particularly impressive at night. With over 300 stores, an art gallery, and even space for a Philharmonic Hall, this retail and entertainment space will keep visitors occupied for hours.

2. Malacca River, Melaka

Next up, make your way to the Melaka River which runs through the city and is the reason why Melaka was given the title 'Venice Of The East' back in the olden days. The river played an extremely important role in Melaka's rich history, welcoming traders from all over the world back in the 1500s. Today, it is lined with many little shops, houses, cafes, restaurants, and buildings splashed with striking graffiti and street art. Hop on a River Cruise for a different perspective of the scenic river or simply spend some time strolling down the banks.

2. Genting Highland, Pahang

One of the most stunning hill stations in Malaysia, Genting Highland is a must-visit place that is perfect to grab a jaw-dropping sight of the dramatic Titiwangsa Mountains. This beautiful haven tucked amidst Mount Ulu Kali serves as an idyllic getaway where you bask in the luxury of resort living, casinos, nature retreats, amusement parks, and whatnot. You can take a tour of the surrounding villages and experience its rustic vibes. Also, get a chance to stroll around the sprawling strawberry farms which literally look like a dream. Located at a distance of 35 km from Kuala Lumpur, Genting is an easily accessible destination as it is well connected through highways, roads, and cable cars via Awana Skyway.

2. George Town, Penang

A famous gastronomy hub and a melting pot of cultures, Penang is one of the most renowned Malaysian tourist places that is a delightful discovery for food lovers and culture buffs alike. Besides its vibrant food scene, the island of Penang is known for its old-world charm. UNESCO World Heritage-listed George Town which brims with colonial-era buildings is one of the top attractions of Penang. Also, the island is an idyllic gateway where you can expect picture-perfect beaches, thriving nightlife, street art scene, nouvelle cuisine, religious sites, and whatnot. So lose yourself in the sublimity of the island, charming old-town vibe, and regional specialties.
